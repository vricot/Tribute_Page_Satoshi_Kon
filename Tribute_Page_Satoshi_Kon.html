Satoshi Kon

Japanese Animation Legend

The Life of Satoshi Kon

  • October 12th, 1963: Born in Sapporo, Hokkaido, Japan

  • Attended Hokkaido Kushiro Koryo High School, where he made close friends with manga artist Seiho Takizawa

  • 1982: Graduated from the Graphic Design course at Musashino Art University

  • 1984: Earned a runner-up spot on 10th Annual Tetsuya Chiba Awards by Young Magazine on his debut short manga Toriko

  • 1987: Graduated from college

  • 1990: Released his one-volume manga Kaikisen and wrote the script for Katsuhiro Otomo's live-action film World Apartment Horror

  • 1993: Scripted and co-produced the fifth episode of original video animation Jojo's Bizarre Adventure

  • 1997: Began work on his directorial debut Perfect Blue, to be produced by Madhouse

  • 2002: Released his second film, Millennium Actress, earning numerous awards

  • 2003: Release of his third film, Tokyo Godfathers

  • 2004: Release of his 13-episode television series, Paranoia Agent

  • 2006: His film Paprika is announced, after several years of planning. The film gathers massive attention

  • 2007: Teamed up with Mamoru Oshii and Makoto Shinkai to create the NHK TV production Ani*Kuri15 (his own short being Ohayo)

  • 2007: Helped establish and served as a member of the Japan Animation Creators Association (JAniCA)

  • May 2010: Diagnosed with terminal pancreatic cancer, with half a year to live. Chose to spend the remainder of his life in his home

  • August 24th, 2010 (Age 46): Died of pancreatic cancer in Tokyo, Japan
